Comparative Team Analysis

At Cloud 11 we have come together as a group from various geographic locations and backgrounds with differing levels of work experience. Each of us wanting to specialise in a different field from the next. Our group has a variety of jobs that we wish to persue for a future career, these jobs share many of the same skillsets.

Breaking down the foundational skills groups:
Digital Building Block skills, these are critical skills that are required for development of IT skills. We typically build on these to create IT-Specific skills.
Human skills, typically referred to as soft skills. These are often overlooked but most employers will typically interview the person before they interview the programmer.
Business Enabler skills, are the skills that allow us to integrate our human skills and technical skills to the workplace environment.

At Cloud 11 we have a Data Specialist and Senior Analyst looking at more frontend positions, with a heavy focus on data analysis, communicating through data visualisation and business processes. While being different on paper the core skills are remarkably similar with these two jobs. Both jobs work around collecting data and presenting it in an understandable manner, with their creativity and data visualisation expertise, to business stakeholders to increase efficiency and augment sales. As these are frontend IT positions the programming knowledge needed is limited and typically it would be statistical programming (Python, R, SQL).

The other four members of the group are heavily focused on backend work. These are roles such as Cyber Security Analyst, Cloud Engineer, App Developer and Network Administrator. All these jobs are heavily focussed on backend operations such as digital security and privacy, networking skills, scripting, programming, and virtualisation. They also have a lot of overlapping skills in all three of the categories.

IT Jobs have such an overlap in skills as the IT industry is full of hybrid jobs. Hybrid jobs are jobs that are assigned two or more roles for the job.
